The Prime Minister's seat is not as comfortable as he'd like. And some people in Uxbridge are feeling indecisive. To help them make up their minds and choose a trustworthy MP, we've taken a closer look to see whether Boris' claims are bona fide or just BS.

On Heathrow expansion

Claim:

“I will lie down with you in front of those bulldozers and stop the building, stop the construction of that third runway.”

Reality:

Despite his past claims to passionately reject the third runway at Heathrow, Mr Johnson abstained on a key vote on Heathrow expansion this year and has had little to say about it since.

On building hospitals

Claim:

“We are building 40 new hospitals.”

Reality:

Mr Johnson has claimed that his party would build 40 new hospitals, but this figure is spurious at best. The number seems to be based on the Conservatives being in power for the next 10 years (so two future election wins), and includes renovations of existing hospitals.

On his Brexit deal

Claim:

“Parliament voted to approve my Brexit deal, then voted for delay.”

Reality:

Parliament has never approved Mr Johnson’s Brexit deal. It only backed a second reading of the Withdrawal Agreement Bill. This often happens to secure a debate, with MPs then voting against a bill at a later point if it isn't amended to be acceptable. Many MPs have said they would have done just that in this case.

On the cost of delaying Brexit

Claim:

"With every month of pointless delay – insisted on by Corbyn – it's costing this country an extra billion pounds."

Reality:

According to fact checking website Full Fact, the conveniently round figure of a billion pounds per month is only compared to a no-deal Brexit, in which the UK pays no ‘divorce bill’. It’s also misleading as it doesn’t include money that the UK gets back from the EU. The reality is that if, as he says, Mr Johnson gets a deal, it will most likely involve continued payments, particularly through the transition period.

...and more...

On Labour's corporation tax plans

Claim:

"We have already cut it from 28 to 19%, the lowest of any major economy. The alternative is Corbyn – who would whack it straight back up to the highest levels in Europe."

Reality:

Labour's plan is to raise corporation tax to 26%. That is still lower than France (32%), Portugal (32%), Germany (30%), Belgium (30%), Greece (28%) and Italy (28%).
